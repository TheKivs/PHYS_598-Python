\section{PHYS 598 SDA RECITATION 10 - PROBLEM SET}


\textbf{1. Conditional distribution:} Discuss the proof of the following theorem (Theorem A.11 in the notes). Let $\bm X = (X_1,\ldots,X_n)$ be a multivariate normal random vector with mean $\bm\mu$, and $\bm X_a = (X_1,\ldots,X_k)$ and $\bm X_b = (X_{k+1},\ldots,X_n)$. Then, the conditional distribution of $\bm X_a$ given $\bm X_b = \bm x_b$ is multivariate normal with mean
\[E[\bm X_a|\bm X_b=\bm x_b] = \bm \mu_a+\bm{\Sigma_{ab}\Sigma_{bb}^{-1}(x_b-\mu_b)}\]
and variance
\[Var[\bm X_a|\bm x_b] = \bm{\Sigma_{aa}-\Sigma_{ab}\Sigma_{bb}^{-1}\Sigma_{ba}} = \bm{\Lambda_{aa}^{-1}}.\]
Are the above answers are intuitive in the limit $k\to 1$ and $k \to n$? \\

Information theory I: } Think about problems 3,4, 5 and 11 from \href{http://www-m5.ma.tum.de/foswiki/pub/M5/Allgemeines/MA5103_2011W/problems.pdf}{this} TU M\"{u}nchen problem set. \emph{R\'{e}nyi entropy (prob 3) is a widely used entropy measure. It is prevalent in the computation of entanglement entropy in QFTs, by resorting to the so-called replica trick. Come talk to me if you like any of the other problems!}\\
